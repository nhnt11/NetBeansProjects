The 2nd Layer

The second layer might be a little easier for some of you. Basically, you have to use the corner that you left empty in the first layer to put in the edges of the middle layer. Take a look below:

Notice how the orange/green edge on the bottom is brought to its proper place by bringing the face in which belongs down, and then matching the edge to the center cube of the same color.

Now take a look at this case in which the edge is already in the middle layer, but not in its correct spot. (It may also be in the correct spot, but flipped over) What you have to do is bring that edge to the bottom layer, and then put it in like normal. Ignore the fact that the empty corner isnt empty anymore, it happens sometimes. Just pretend its still empty.

Put in all the middle layer edges, and your cube will look something like this:

Now, you need to put in the empty corner again. If the white sticker is not on the bottom face, then you need to align it diagonally opposite to the empty corner such that it isnt visible if you look at that face straight on. Then, bring that white piece to the top so that 2 white pieces are 'hanging off' and move the bottom twice. Then bring the 2 hanging white pices back up and put in the corner. This will put in both the corner and the edge at the same time. Here is what I mean:

Congratulations! You are now half done with the cube. You may be thinking, "Hey! I'm two thirds done!" But the bottom layer is a little more complicated, so you can consider it to be half the cube. This is because you cant mess up anything that you've already done when solving the bottom layer, so the help of slightly complicated algorithms is required. Good luck!
